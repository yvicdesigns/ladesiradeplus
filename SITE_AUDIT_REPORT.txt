COMPLETE SITE AUDIT REPORT - KEY'S FOOD APPLICATION
Date: March 1, 2026

SECTION 1: WHAT THIS SITE DOES
This application is a complete digital platform for a restaurant, allowing customers to easily order food for delivery or dine-in, book tables, and track their orders in real-time. It is used by hungry customers looking for a convenient way to get their meals, and by the restaurant staff and management to efficiently handle incoming orders, track inventory, and analyze business performance. 

SECTION 2: MAIN FEATURES & PAGES
- Home/Landing Page: The welcoming front door of the app. It highlights top dishes, guides users to order or book a table, and adapts based on whether the user is a customer or a manager. It works very well and looks modern.
- Menu & Product Browsing: Where customers view the food. It supports categories, stock limits (showing "sold out" when empty), and user reviews. It functions smoothly and updates in real-time.
- Shopping Cart: Holds the selected items before purchase. It successfully calculates totals, applies promo codes, and warns if an item suddenly goes out of stock.
- Checkout & Payment: The final step to place an order. Customers can choose delivery (using GPS, typed address, or neighborhood) or dine-in (table number). It calculates delivery fees based on distance perfectly.
- Order Tracking: A live page where customers watch their order move from "Pending" to "Preparing" to "Delivered." It includes voice alerts to notify them of changes. It works reliably.
- User Accounts & Profiles: Customers can save their details (name, phone, address) so they don't have to type them every time. Staff can access elevated permissions here.
- Reservations System: Allows customers to book a table for a specific date and time. It works well and notifies the staff instantly.
- Admin Dashboard: The command center for the owner. Shows daily revenue, order counts, and live activities. Very well-designed and stable.
- Restaurant Order Management: A specific screen for staff to manage dine-in orders and update kitchen statuses.
- Delivery Management: A screen to assign and track deliveries, manage delivery zones, and update prices. 
- Customer Management: A database of all people who have ordered, showing how much they've spent and how often they visit.
- Settings & Configuration: Where the owner can change the restaurant logo, business hours, and sounds.

SECTION 3: PAYMENT SYSTEM
- How customers pay: Customers can pay via Cash on Delivery, Wallet, MTN Mobile Money, or Airtel Money.
- Payment processing flow: Currently, the system uses a "manual verification" flow for mobile money.
- Mobile money integration: Customers select MTN or Airtel, make the transfer on their phone, and then MUST upload a screenshot (proof of payment).
- Limitations: Because mobile money isn't directly connected to the telecom company's automatic system, a human staff member must look at the uploaded screenshot and click "Approve" before the order is fully confirmed.

SECTION 4: USER AUTHENTICATION & ACCOUNTS
- Sign up / Log in: Handled securely via email and password using a top-tier security provider (Supabase).
- Stored data: Names, emails, phone numbers, delivery addresses, and order history.
- Security: Passwords are encrypted. Users can only see their own orders.
- User Roles: Customers (normal users), Staff (can manage orders), Managers (can manage inventory/staff), Admins (can delete things and change system settings).
- Issues: Authentication is very solid, no major issues found.

SECTION 5: ADMIN FEATURES
- Capabilities: Admins can update the menu, change prices, view financial reports, manage staff roles, and see every order in real-time.
- Reporting features: Excellent charts showing revenue over the last 7 days and average basket sizes.
- Settings: High level of control, down to customizing the automated voice that reads out orders.
- Issues: The sheer amount of data could become slightly overwhelming if thousands of orders are placed in a single day, but filters help manage this.

SECTION 6: DATA & DATABASE
- Stored info: Food items, categories, exact order details, delivery driver locations, and customer reviews.
- Retention: Data is kept indefinitely unless manually deleted or archived by an admin.
- Privacy: Good separation of data. Only authorized staff can view customer phone numbers and addresses.
- Backup: The system has an Admin Backup page to manually or automatically save a snapshot of the data.

SECTION 7: WHAT WORKS WELL
1. Real-time Order Tracking: Customers instantly see when their food is ready.
2. GPS Delivery Fee Calculator: Automatically figures out how much to charge based on the customer's exact location.
3. Inventory Protection: Automatically stops customers from buying food that just ran out of stock.
4. Voice Notifications: The kitchen tablet literally speaks out loud when a new order arrives ("Nouvelle commande reçue").
5. Promo Code System: Accurately applies percentage or fixed discounts and tracks usage limits.
6. Design: The orange and white color scheme is very appetizing and easy to navigate on mobile phones.

SECTION 8: POTENTIAL ISSUES & BUGS
1. Payment Verification Bottleneck: Because mobile money relies on screenshot uploads, if the restaurant is very busy, staff might take too long to verify the images, frustrating customers.
2. Audio Autoplay Blocks: Browsers (like Chrome/Safari) sometimes block the "Voice Notifications" until the admin actually clicks somewhere on the screen.
3. Complex Status Synchronization: Sometimes a dine-in order and its specific kitchen items have separate statuses. If staff forget to update both, reports might look slightly off.
4. Offline Mode: While it detects when internet is lost, the app cannot process new orders without an active connection.

SECTION 9: MISSING FEATURES
1. Automated Payment Gateway: Direct connection to MTN/Airtel so screenshots are no longer needed.
2. Dedicated Driver App: Currently, drivers might just use the web link. A separate, simplified mobile app for drivers tracking routes would be great.
3. Automated Refunds: If an order is canceled, staff must manually send the money back via mobile money; the system doesn't do it automatically.

SECTION 10: SECURITY ASSESSMENT
- Authentication: Excellent. Supabase handles this securely.
- Data Protection: Strong. Row Level Security (RLS) ensures hackers cannot download the customer database.
- Payment Security: Safe, because no credit card numbers are stored (using manual transfers).
- Admin Access: Strictly enforced. A normal user simply cannot load the admin pages.
- Recommendations: Keep monitoring the uploaded payment screenshots to ensure malicious files aren't uploaded (currently restricted to images, which is good).

SECTION 11: PERFORMANCE & SPEED
- Page Load Times: Very fast. The application loads different pieces only when needed (Lazy Loading).
- Real-time features: Extremely responsive. Status changes appear on the customer's phone in milliseconds.
- Overall speed: Great. However, if the menu grows to 1000+ items, the images should be compressed to keep the home page fast.

SECTION 12: OVERALL HEALTH SCORE
- Functionality: 9/10
- Stability: 8/10
- Security: 9/10
- Performance: 8/10
- User Experience: 9/10
- OVERALL HEALTH: 8.6/10 (Excellent)

SECTION 13: TOP RECOMMENDATIONS
1. Critical Fixes: Train staff specifically on the "Screenshot Verification" process so orders aren't delayed. 
2. Important additions: Invest in an automated Mobile Money API (like CinetPay or direct telecom API) to remove the screenshot upload step entirely.
3. User Experience: Add a "Re-order my last meal" button for returning customers to make ordering take 5 seconds.
4. Business Growth: Use the built-in "Marketing" tab to send SMS or Email promotions to customers who haven't ordered in 30 days.
5. Security: Ensure all staff accounts have strong passwords, as they have access to customer addresses.

SECTION 14: SUMMARY
The Key's Food application is highly advanced, beautifully designed, and functionally robust. It completely digitizes the restaurant's operations from the kitchen to the delivery driver. The site is absolutely ready for customers to use right now. The only main area needing attention is replacing the manual "screenshot for payment" system with an automated payment gateway in the future to handle higher volumes of sales efficiently. Next steps should be a soft-launch with real customers to test the kitchen's reaction time to the digital alerts.
